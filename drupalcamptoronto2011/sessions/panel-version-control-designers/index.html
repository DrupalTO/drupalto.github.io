Panel: Version Control for Designers

Session Track: 
Business and Strategy
Difficulty: 
Beginner

There has been a lot of discussion over different methods of version control for Web Designers. Developers have had the benefits of using Git for collaborative works for some time now, but there has yet to be a strong use-case for incorporating git into traditional design techniques.

Mojo Design is a design collective of traditionally-trained graphic designers. Using the open-source version control of Git and SVG Illustrator files, they have been collaboratively designing brands and websites.

This panel intends to bridge the gap of traditional and experimental design techniques by demonstrating an actual use-case of version-controlled design. These designers will discuss the benefits and draw-backs of using version control for collaborative design, while also demonstrating that version control is not just for developers.

About the Panel
Mojo is a design collective made of multi-talented and multi-interested graduates of the graphic design program at OCAD U looking to reclaim all the passion and spark that university has taken away from us. Mojo is our creative refuge, a visual playground to explore, sharing our toolsets, pulling together resources and collaborating with other designers and artists.
